Here's a couple of things to do on your social media and technology devices to stay safer:


    1.Two-Step Verification
    2.Back-up Data

Two-Step Verification is a simple thing to opt into which prevents hackers from getting into your account using a single password. It lets your secondary recovery method to able to input a software as well. This makes the hackers job nearly impossible unless both devices/accounts are stolen. Back-up Data, at home, helps for not only protecting your device, but for transferring information between devices. It allows a previous version of your potentially stolen data to be restored. Although previously mentioned, constantly updating a software can protect from the attacks that update right with your Antivirus.



what to watch for


Ransomware is a type of malware using an attacker holding the victim's files "hostage", usually via encryption, and demanding a payment to get them back. Using one of these recovery plans we can regain the lost data. The simplest way to attack someone is through Social Engineering. Social engineering is an attack that relies on human interaction to trick users into breaking security procedures in order to gain sensitive information that is typically protected. This is usually used by promising a service or product in exchange for this info. Malware, one common but not the easiest ways of harming a computer, is software that enters a computer through accidental downloads. Using worms, Trojan horses, or basic computer viruses they can get into a computer's files. Another software that is more dangerous than just accessing files is Spyware. Spyware watches your computer and keystrokes where the hacker can find your password. Different types of spyware are password stealers which collect data of your passwords and can access your accounts using those passwords. Another virus is Adware. Adware is the process of embedding a virus into an advertisement hence the name. You have probably been targeted by adware when you get a message saying, "You have won $10,000, click here to claim your prize" or subtle variations, even when you have not signed up for something. The easiest way to avoid this is to stay away from random, unusual advertisements.



email


According to the Federal Communications Commission, over 60% of email is documented on spam. Email is the primary method for spreading viruses and malware. Sending fake email pretending to be trustworthy is Phishing. Phishing is a form of fraud where fraudulent emails are sent that resemble emails from reputable sources; however, the intention of these emails is to steal sensitive data, such as credit card or login information. You can easily prevent this by using an email filtering service. Some email providers already provide this.

When sending sensitive information in an email, try to use an encrypting service to protect that information. Encryption is the process of converting data into an unreadable format to prevent disclosure to unauthorized personnel. Email itself isn't always the most secure.



recovery


Moving on, disasters can be recovered from quickly although it is a little unfamiliar. A Disaster Recovery Plan(DRP) or a Business Continuity Plan(BCP) are common in businesses to reverse the effects of the damage caused. These documents are structured approaches with instructions for responding to unplanned incidents. It usually involves back-ups of previous versions of software or documents. BCP's and DRP's are most effective against Ransomware.
